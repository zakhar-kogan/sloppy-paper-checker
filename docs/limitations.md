# Limitations and responsible interpretation

PDF.js text extraction can fail or lose reading order on scans, unusual multi-column layouts, mathematical notation, figures, and tables. Encrypted, malformed, image-only, over-25-MB, and over-300-page PDFs are rejected with an explicit message. PMC JATS usually provides stronger paragraph anchors but can omit publisher-only material. Supplements are listed when discovered and are not assessed.

Abstract-only and metadata-only analyses deliberately disable unsupported methodology checks. Their numeric Review score summarizes only assessed eligible items and must be read beside both coverage measures. Even a complete full-text report can miss domain-specific flaws.

Worker and adjudicator models can misunderstand methods. Workers retrieve evidence but do not grade it; the stronger adjudicator reads the normalized paper and assigns every final grade. Constrained final output, exact-quote grounding, fixed expected items, deterministic validation, and visible failure states reduce unsupported output but do not eliminate it. The adjudicator can cite new evidence from the supplied paper but cannot retrieve cited work.

English is validated first; other languages are experimental. Resolver metadata and publication-record adapters can be incomplete or stale. Record status is contextual and does not mathematically change the score.

Do not use the software to automate editorial rejection, rank people, make clinical or policy decisions, or publish allegations. Open the cited spans, verify the latest paper version, consult domain experts, and use the relevant validated appraisal instrument for consequential decisions.
