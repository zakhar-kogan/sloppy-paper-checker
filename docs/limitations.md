# Limitations and responsible interpretation

Scientific assessment is field-specific and frequently underdetermined by a paper alone. Extraction can fail on scans, unusual layouts, mathematical notation, tables, supplementary files, or authenticated content. English is validated first; other languages are experimental. Common-core analysis of unsupported paper types explicitly leaves specialist dimensions unassessed.

Automated identity resolution can collide names. Author, grant, payment, affiliation, citation, and journal datasets have uneven geographic and disciplinary coverage. Missing records are neutral. Repeated coauthorship is descriptive. Comments, corrections, and affiliations do not by themselves establish misconduct, bias, or invalidity.

Language models can misread methods and citations. The evidence schema and critic reduce unsupported output but do not eliminate it. Readers should open every cited span/source, check the latest paper version, consult domain experts, and use the relevant validated appraisal instrument for consequential decisions.

The software should not be used to rank people, automate editorial rejection, make clinical decisions, or publish accusations. Evaluation tracks unsupported-finding rate independently from ordinary code coverage.
